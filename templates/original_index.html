    Data Analysts in Germany

    This is a report on the current state of employment of data analysts in Germany. The data was obtained from the German job search website: www.stepstone.de. 5000 data were obtained through a python program and being analyzed in different ways with different tools, in order to have a perspective of the job market in Germany as a Data Analyst.

    Part 1: Overall Perspective

    Judging by the number of posted job on Stepstone, it is not hard to conclude that there is a quite high demand for Data Analyst in german job market. Almost all of them offer permanent contacts and require full-time presence, although there is about 40% of chance that you can work from home.

    During the week that is being surveyed, there were an average of 283 job with the title "Data Analyst" posted on Stepstone. Of these, Friday,Saturday and Sunday have less postings, which are below 300. While from Monday to Thursday there are more than 300 Data Analyst job were posted every day.

    Of the 5,000 jobs surveyed, 87.52% offered permanent contracts, 3% offered fix-term contacts, 9.24% offered contracts to students, including 3.86% of Student jobs and 1.16% of Ausbildung (a special kind of internship in Germany).The rest of 4.42% are offered for interns.

    Of the 5,000 jobs surveyed, Of the 5,000 jobs surveyed, 40% of the positions are home office friendly, 2% even encourage you to do so, however 58% of the positions are not clear on whether they liked the idea of home office or not, but I suppose you could negotiate.

    Part 2:About Employer

    In which industry are data analysts most popular? Where are the companies that employ data analysts located in Germany?

    There is always a place for data analysts in many industries, but of course, it is still the IT industry that holds the overwhelming majority of positions. Among the most in-demand positions in the IT industry are, database development and management, software development, consulting, and business informatics. The second-ranked industry is finance, which includes business analysis and control. Then followed by some engineering management involving technology, as well as marketing analysis, team management, banking and insurance, and research in universities, etc.

    Geographical distribution of jobs in Germany

    The most popular cities for data analysts in Germany are, Munich, Stuttgart, Frankfurt(am Main), Berlin, Köln, Hamburg, of course, no surprise, the big cities.

    Part 3: Employment

    Who are being hired? What are the required skills to become a data analyst? How is the salary distribution like?

    Required Skills

    Becoming a data analyst requires professional skills, as well as some soft skills. The profession includes both technical and business aspects. On the technical aspect, first, a university degree related to mathematics, computer science and economics are required, and a mastery of software related skills such as python, SQL, MS Office, etc. is also very necessary. For the soft skills, fluency in German and English will give you a big advantage in the competition, and a flexible personality and an analytical mind will give you more chances to get hired.

    We divided the data analyst jobs into three categories according to their industries, IT, finance and Engineering and Technology.The data analysts in Finance have the highest average salaries, but their salaries also have a greater range of variation, the second highest average salary is in Engineering and Technology. The data analysts in the IT category have the lowest average salary, but also the smallest range of variation.

    Wordcloud of the Job Description

    After analyzing the 5,000 job descriptions obtained, we extracted the most important keywords using a word cloud and obtained two reports. The left one stands for all the required skills in 5000 descriptions of the job positions, you can see that a degree of business, computer science are very important, as well as German, English are also highly demanded skills.
